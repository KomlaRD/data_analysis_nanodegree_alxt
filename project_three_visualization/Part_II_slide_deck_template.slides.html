Part II - Factors associated with Diabetes among females of Pima Indian Heritage¶

by Eric Anku¶

Investigation Overview¶

This investigation explored factors associated with diabetes among females of Pima Indian Heritage 21 years and older. Participants with diabetes were on average older than participants without diabetes. The chances of diabetes increased with increasing number of pregnancies, blood pressure, triceps skinfold thinckness and BMI. Participants with diabetes had higher amounts of serum insulin compared to participants without diabetes and a higher Diabetes Pedigree Funtion on average was also associated with increased chances of having diabetes. Participants with diabetes had higher blood glucose 2 hours after oral glucose test.

Dataset Overview¶

The dataset consisted of 768 observation with 9 features. The main outcome in the dataset was diabetes among females of Pima Indian Heritage. Explanatory features in the dataset includes number of pregnancies, serum glucose, insulin, blood pressure, tricep skinfold thickness, BMI, Diabetes Pedigree Function, and age.
